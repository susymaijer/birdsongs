About Bird Songs International

Bird Songs International BV was founded in the Netherlands in 1996. It was formed by Sjoerd Mayer and Bernard Geling to publish Sjoerd's CD-ROM Bird Sounds of Bolivia / Sonidos de Aves de Bolivia 1.0. Heartened by its good reception, we continued to publish Birds of Tropical Asia by Jelle Scharringa and its successors Birds of Tropical Asia 2 and Birds of Tropical Asia 3, Peter Boesman's Birds of Venezuela / Aves de Venezuela, the second version of the Bolivia CD-ROM Birds of Bolivia / Aves de Bolivia 2.0, and Birds of Ecuador / Aves de Ecuador by Niels Krabbe and Jonas Nilsson.

In 2000 we started adding other bird sound publications to our shop. It didn't take long until we offered a very wide range of bird sound CDs, cassettes and CD-ROMs, covering the whole world.

Everything went smoothly: the shop was a money spinner, the customers were happy, and so were we. Or so I thought. So when I made a detailed and mutually beneficial proposal for Bernard for him to buy my 80% of the shares (I was going to receive a disability "pension" from 2005 onwards because of my illness), I expected it to be the start of talks between us about my proposal, price, conditions, etc. That was in July 2004. Instead he went practically silent, and left me completely in the dark about what he was up to. Eventually, without any warning (except saying one day in a mysterious voice, when I asked why he was behaving so strangely: "I know what I am doing"), at the end of 2004 Bernard Geling forced me to buy his shares in the company, took a big chunk of our inventory with him (despite signing an agreement that he would hand over everything, in return for me buying his shares), and started his own shop with bird sounds stuff. So much for 30-year friendships! In July 2005 he sent me a note telling me why he did it: "advisers" told him to, without naming them (a better explanation emerges from the following: a few years ago Annemiek -his partner- told me she was surprised that Bernard and I were getting along so well for already such a long time. I asked why. "Because", she said, "he always ends up fighting with everybody"). I hope that you, mr./mrs. visitor, don't have someone like Bernard Geling among your friends.

Unfortunately I am ill with multiple sclerosis (I'm in a wheelchair, my hands are getting worse, I'm tired like a dog most of the time, and all that). So thus came an end to a wonderful little enterprise, a one-day-of-work-a-week, EUR 25,000 profit a year, smoothly running little money machine. Bernard's actions forced me to run it single-handedly in 2005 (quite literally, as the MS already made my left hand half-limp. Fortunately my wife Nieves helped me pack the boxes), but I had to wind the enterprise down, otherwise I would have lost the pension entirely (having lost it already in 2005, because of too much profit). Fortunately I managed to wind it down in an elegant way: I sold my inventory to Wildsounds in August 2005, and I closed the shop completely in December.

Sjoerd Mayer
